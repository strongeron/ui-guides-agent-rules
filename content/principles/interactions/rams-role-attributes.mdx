<Callout type="quote" title="WCAG 4.1.2 - Level A">
  For all user interface components, the name and role can be programmatically determined. ARIA roles have required attributes - using a role without its required attributes creates broken, unusable components for assistive technology users.
</Callout>

## Why This Matters

ARIA roles come with contracts. A checkbox must have aria-checked. A slider must have aria-valuenow, aria-valuemin, and aria-valuemax. Missing required attributes mean the role is announced but the state is unknown - like a checkbox that never tells you if it's checked.

## Implementation

<CodeBlock title="HTML/JSX" language="html">
{`<!-- checkbox requires aria-checked -->
<div
  role="checkbox"
  aria-checked={isChecked}
  tabIndex={0}
>
  <CheckIcon />
</div>

<!-- slider requires aria-valuenow, min, max -->
<div
  role="slider"
  aria-valuenow={50}
  aria-valuemin={0}
  aria-valuemax={100}
  aria-label="Volume"
  tabIndex={0}
/>

<!-- tablist/tab/tabpanel pattern -->
<div role="tablist">
  <button role="tab" aria-selected={true} aria-controls="panel-1">
    Tab 1
  </button>
</div>
<div role="tabpanel" id="panel-1" aria-labelledby="tab-1">
  Content
</div>`}
</CodeBlock>
