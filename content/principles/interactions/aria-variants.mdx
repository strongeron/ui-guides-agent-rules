<Callout type="quote" title="Tailwind Golden Rule">
  Use Tailwind's aria-* variants (aria-selected:, aria-expanded:, aria-disabled:) to style based on ARIA state, keeping styles in sync with accessibility attributes.
</Callout>

## Why This Matters

ARIA attributes communicate state to assistive technologies. Using `aria-*` variants ensures your visual styles match the accessibility state. This prevents mismatches where something *looks* selected but isn't *announced* as selected, or vice versa.

## Implementation

<CodeBlock title="Tailwind Classes" language="html">
{`<!-- Tab with aria-selected styling -->
<button
  role="tab"
  aria-selected="true"
  class="aria-selected:bg-primary aria-selected:text-primary-foreground"
>
  Tab 1
</button>

<!-- Accordion trigger -->
<button
  aria-expanded="false"
  class="aria-expanded:rotate-180"
>
  <ChevronIcon />
</button>

<!-- Disabled state -->
<button
  aria-disabled="true"
  class="aria-disabled:opacity-50 aria-disabled:cursor-not-allowed"
>`}
</CodeBlock>
