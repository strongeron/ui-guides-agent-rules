<Callout type="quote" title="WCAG 1.1.1 - Level A">
  All non-text content must have a text alternative that serves the equivalent purpose. Images without alt text are invisible to screen reader users.
</Callout>

## Why This Matters

Screen reader users rely on alt text to understand images. Missing alt text means the image is either skipped entirely or the filename is read aloud (e.g., "IMG_2847.jpg"), providing no useful information. Decorative images should have an empty `alt=""` to be properly ignored.

## Implementation

<CodeBlock title="HTML" language="html">
{`<!-- Informative image - describe the content -->
<img src="/team-photo.jpg" alt="Team members at the 2024 company retreat" />

<!-- Functional image (link/button) - describe the action -->
<button>
  <img src="/search-icon.svg" alt="Search" />
</button>

<!-- Decorative image - empty alt to hide from AT -->
<img src="/decorative-border.png" alt="" />

<!-- Complex image - use aria-describedby for long descriptions -->
<figure>
  <img
    src="/chart.png"
    alt="Q3 revenue chart"
    aria-describedby="chart-desc"
  />
  <figcaption id="chart-desc">
    Revenue grew 23% from $2.1M to $2.6M...
  </figcaption>
</figure>`}
</CodeBlock>
