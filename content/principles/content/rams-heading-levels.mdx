<Callout type="quote" title="WCAG 1.3.1 - Level A">
  Information and relationships conveyed through presentation can be programmatically determined. Heading levels create a document outline - skipping levels (h1 to h3) breaks this outline for assistive technology users.
</Callout>

## Why This Matters

Screen reader users navigate by headings to understand page structure. Jumping from h1 to h3 (skipping h2) creates a confusing, broken outline. They may think content is missing. Use CSS for visual styling - headings convey structure, not size.

## Implementation

<CodeBlock title="HTML/JSX" language="html">
{`<!-- Correct heading hierarchy -->
<h1>Page Title</h1>
  <h2>Main Section</h2>
    <h3>Subsection</h3>
    <h3>Another Subsection</h3>
  <h2>Another Main Section</h2>
    <h3>Subsection</h3>

<!-- Style with CSS, not heading level -->
<h2 className="text-lg font-medium">Section Title</h2>
<h3 className="text-base font-medium">Subsection</h3>

<!-- Single h1 per page -->
<main>
  <h1>Product Features</h1>
  <!-- Not another h1 -->
</main>`}
</CodeBlock>
