<Callout type="quote" title="WCAG 1.4.3 - Level AA">
  Normal text needs at least 4.5:1 contrast ratio. Large text (18px+ bold or 24px+ regular) needs 3:1 minimum. Low contrast text is difficult or impossible to read for users with low vision.
</Callout>

## Why This Matters

Poor text contrast affects everyone - in bright sunlight, on low-quality displays, or with any vision impairment. Gray text on white backgrounds is a common violation. Always check contrast ratios, especially for secondary/muted text which is often made too light.

## Implementation

<CodeBlock title="CSS/Tailwind" language="html">
{`<!-- Good contrast for body text -->
<p className="text-foreground">Primary content</p>  <!-- ~15:1 -->

<!-- Muted but still readable -->
<p className="text-muted-foreground">Secondary text</p>  <!-- ~7:1 -->

<!-- Check placeholders meet contrast -->
<input placeholder="Search..." className="placeholder:text-muted-foreground" />

<!-- Large text can have lower contrast -->
<h1 className="text-4xl text-muted-foreground">Heading</h1>  <!-- 3:1 OK -->

<!-- Tools to check:
     - Chrome DevTools > Elements > Contrast ratio
     - WebAIM Contrast Checker
     - Figma A11y plugins -->`}
</CodeBlock>
