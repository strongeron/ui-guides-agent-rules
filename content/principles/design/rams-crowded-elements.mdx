<Callout type="quote" title="Design System Principle">
  Crowded elements are hard to read, hard to tap, and feel overwhelming. Adequate spacing improves readability, tap accuracy, and visual hierarchy.
</Callout>

## Why This Matters

When elements are too close together, they compete for attention and become harder to distinguish. Text needs line-height for readability. Buttons need padding for tap targets. Cards need margins to separate them visually. Crowding undermines usability.

## Implementation

<CodeBlock title="CSS/Tailwind" language="html">
{`<!-- Adequate padding in buttons -->
<button className="px-4 py-2">Submit</button>

<!-- Adequate line-height in text -->
<p className="leading-relaxed">Long paragraph text...</p>

<!-- Adequate space between form fields -->
<form className="space-y-4">
  <Input />
  <Input />
  <Button />
</form>

<!-- Adequate padding in cards -->
<div className="p-6">
  <h3 className="mb-2">Title</h3>
  <p className="mb-4">Description</p>
  <Button />
</div>`}
</CodeBlock>
