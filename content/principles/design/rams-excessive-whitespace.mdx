<Callout type="quote" title="Design System Principle">
  While whitespace is valuable, excessive spacing breaks visual connections between related elements and wastes precious viewport space. Balance is key.
</Callout>

## Why This Matters

Too much whitespace disconnects related content - users lose the association between a label and its field, or between a heading and its content. It also wastes screen real estate, especially problematic on mobile. The goal is appropriate spacing that groups related content while separating distinct sections.

## Implementation

<CodeBlock title="CSS/Tailwind" language="html">
{`<!-- Appropriate spacing hierarchy -->
<div className="space-y-8">  <!-- Larger between sections -->
  <section>
    <h2 className="mb-4">Section Title</h2>  <!-- Medium after heading -->
    <div className="space-y-3">  <!-- Smaller between items -->
      <Item />
      <Item />
    </div>
  </section>
</div>

<!-- Form field - label close to input -->
<div className="space-y-1.5">  <!-- 6px keeps label connected -->
  <label>Email</label>
  <input />
</div>

<!-- NOT this (too much) -->
<div className="space-y-8">
  <label>Email</label>  <!-- Label feels disconnected -->
  <input />
</div>`}
</CodeBlock>
