<Callout type="quote" title="Design System Principle">
  Colors should form a harmonious palette derived from your design system. Random or clashing colors create visual discord and undermine your brand.
</Callout>

## Why This Matters

Clashing colors distract users and make interfaces feel chaotic or amateurish. A harmonious palette (analogous, complementary, or from a unified design system) creates visual comfort and professionalism. Stick to your design tokens and avoid one-off color values.

## Implementation

<CodeBlock title="CSS/Tailwind" language="html">
{`<!-- Use design tokens, not arbitrary colors -->
<button className="bg-primary text-primary-foreground">
  Branded action
</button>

<!-- Semantic colors for consistency -->
<div className="text-success">Success message</div>
<div className="text-destructive">Error message</div>
<div className="text-warning">Warning message</div>

<!-- Background/foreground pairs -->
<div className="bg-muted text-muted-foreground">
  Secondary content
</div>

<!-- AVOID: arbitrary hex values -->
<!-- bg-[#ff6b35] - random orange -->
<!-- text-[#2ecc71] - random green -->`}
</CodeBlock>
