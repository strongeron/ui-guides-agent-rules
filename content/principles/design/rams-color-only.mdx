<Callout type="quote" title="WCAG 1.4.1 - Level A">
  Color is not used as the only visual means of conveying information, indicating an action, prompting a response, or distinguishing a visual element. Users who are colorblind or using monochrome displays need additional cues.
</Callout>

## Why This Matters

About 8% of men and 0.5% of women have some form of color vision deficiency. If you indicate errors only with red text, required fields only with color, or status only with colored dots, these users miss critical information. Always provide additional visual cues: icons, patterns, text labels, or underlines.

## Implementation

<CodeBlock title="HTML/JSX" language="html">
{`<!-- Error: Use icon + text, not just red -->
<span className="text-destructive flex items-center gap-1">
  <AlertIcon aria-hidden="true" />
  Invalid email address
</span>

<!-- Status: Use icon + label, not just colored dot -->
<span className="flex items-center gap-2">
  <CheckIcon className="text-success" aria-hidden="true" />
  <span>Active</span>
</span>

<!-- Required fields: Add asterisk or text -->
<label>
  Email <span className="text-destructive">*</span>
  <span className="sr-only">(required)</span>
</label>

<!-- Chart data: Use patterns in addition to colors -->
<svg>
  <pattern id="stripes" />
  <rect fill="url(#stripes)" />
</svg>`}
</CodeBlock>
