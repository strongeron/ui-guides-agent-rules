<Callout type="quote" title="Design System Principle">
  Shadows indicate elevation and should follow a consistent system. Mixing different shadow styles (hard vs. soft, colored vs. neutral) creates visual confusion.
</Callout>

## Why This Matters

Shadows communicate depth and layering. Inconsistent shadows confuse the visual hierarchy - users can't tell what's on top of what. A consistent shadow scale (sm, md, lg, xl) with consistent color and blur creates predictable, understandable elevation.

## Implementation

<CodeBlock title="CSS/Tailwind" language="html">
{`<!-- Tailwind shadow scale -->
shadow-sm: Subtle lift (cards, inputs)
shadow: Standard elevation (dropdowns)
shadow-md: Medium elevation (popovers)
shadow-lg: High elevation (modals)
shadow-xl: Highest elevation (dialogs)

<!-- Consistent usage by component type -->
<Card className="shadow-sm" />        /* Base cards */
<Dropdown className="shadow-md" />    /* Floating menus */
<Modal className="shadow-lg" />       /* Dialogs */

<!-- Interaction elevation -->
<Card className="shadow-sm hover:shadow-md transition-shadow">
  Lifts slightly on hover
</Card>

<!-- AVOID: Mixed shadow colors/styles -->
<!-- shadow-[0_0_10px_red] on one card -->
<!-- shadow-xl on another card at same level -->`}
</CodeBlock>
