## Why This Matters

Most interfaces are forgettable. They follow conventions perfectly, use standard
components correctly, and disappear from memory the moment users close the tab.
The difference between good and great design is often a single element that lodges
in the viewer's mind.

Think of the interfaces you remember: Apple's smooth scroll momentum, Stripe's
animated gradient backgrounds, Linear's cursor trails. These are not comprehensive
redesigns - they are singular signature elements executed exceptionally well.

## Finding Your Memorable Element

Ask yourself: "What's the one thing someone will remember about this design?"

If you cannot answer that question clearly, you need to add a signature element:

- **Animated borders**: Gradient conic-gradient animations that draw the eye
- **Distinctive illustrations**: Custom artwork that no one else has
- **Dramatic typography**: Oversized headlines, unexpected font choices
- **Unexpected shadows**: Colored shadows, dramatic depth
- **Micro-interactions**: Hover states that surprise and delight
- **Custom cursors**: Contextual cursor changes on key elements

## Best Practices

- Pick ONE memorable element, not many - multiple "signature" elements compete
- The element should be visible early, ideally above the fold
- It should feel intentional, not random decoration
- Motion-safe: wrap animations in `motion-safe:` for accessibility
- The memorable element should reinforce your brand, not distract from it

## What Not to Do

- Do not add animation everywhere - that is noise, not signal
- Do not rely on stock illustrations - they are forgettable by definition
- Do not copy another brand's signature element - it is not yours
- Do not make the memorable element inaccessible (auto-playing video, seizure-inducing animation)
