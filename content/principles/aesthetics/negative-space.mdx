## Why This Matters

Negative space is not empty space—it is active design. When every pixel is
filled with content, users feel overwhelmed. The eye has nowhere to rest.
Cramped layouts feel cheap and unprofessional.

Generous whitespace creates visual hierarchy, improves readability, and
signals quality. The best designs use negative space as deliberately as
positive elements.

## Best Practices

- Use generous padding within cards and containers (16-24px minimum)
- Increase line-height for body text (1.5-1.75 for readability)
- Add meaningful space between content sections (24-48px)
- Let headings breathe with extra margin-top
- Balance button padding—never cram text into tight boxes
- Consider vertical rhythm: consistent spacing multiples (4, 8, 16, 24, 32px)

## Common Mistakes

- Treating whitespace as wasted space to be filled
- Using minimal padding to "fit more content"
- Tight line-height that makes text hard to read
- Elements touching or nearly touching each other
- Inconsistent spacing that creates visual noise
