## Why This Matters

When every design looks the same, nothing stands out. Templates that use identical
fonts, colors, and layouts across all projects create visual monotony. Users stop
noticing, brands blur together, and the work becomes forgettable.

Variety is memorable. A dark, bold design feels entirely different from an elegant,
light one. Each aesthetic direction creates its own emotional resonance and brand
identity. The goal is not randomness, but intentional differentiation.

## Best Practices

- **Vary themes**: Alternate between dark, light, and colored backgrounds
- **Mix font personalities**: Monospace for technical, serifs for elegant, rounded sans for playful
- **Different border treatments**: Sharp corners, soft radii, heavy borders, no borders
- **Color temperature shifts**: Cool blues and grays vs. warm ambers and creams
- **Density variations**: Dense and compact vs. airy and spacious

## Patterns to Avoid

- Using the same font stack (Inter, Space Grotesk) for every project
- Always defaulting to white backgrounds with blue accents
- Identical border-radius values across all designs
- Same card layout patterns repeated everywhere
