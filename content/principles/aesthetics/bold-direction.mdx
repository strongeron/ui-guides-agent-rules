## Why This Matters

Every AI-generated landing page looks the same: purple-to-blue gradients,
floating orbs, excessive border-radius, buzzwords like "unlock" and "supercharge."
These aren't design decisions—they're defaults.

Real design is choosing a direction—brutalist, organic, retrofuturism, Swiss—
and executing it with conviction. A committed direction, even if polarizing,
is more memorable than safe median aesthetics.

## Best Practices

- Choose a design direction that matches your brand's personality
- Execute consistently—every detail should reinforce the direction
- Avoid mixing directions (brutalist buttons with playful illustrations)
- Let constraints create coherence (limited palette, type scale, radius)
- Polarizing is better than forgettable

## Design Directions to Consider

- **Brutalist**: Raw, functional, monospace, high contrast
- **Editorial**: Magazine-inspired, elegant typography, generous whitespace
- **Retro**: Specific era reference, period-accurate colors and shapes
- **Organic**: Soft shapes, natural colors, handmade textures
- **Swiss**: Grid-based, Helvetica, clean, systematic
