## Why This Matters

Perfectly symmetrical grids are the path of least resistance. They're safe,
predictable, and instantly forgettable. When every feature section uses the
same 3-column layout with identical card sizes, nothing stands out.

Intentional grid-breaking isn't chaos---it's strategic composition. By varying
card sizes, creating diagonal visual flow, and allowing elements to overlap,
you establish hierarchy and guide the viewer's eye through the content.

## Best Practices

- **One dominant element**: Make one card significantly larger to establish hierarchy
- **Diagonal flow**: Arrange elements so the eye moves diagonally across the layout
- **Intentional overlap**: Use z-index and absolute positioning to create depth
- **Varied proportions**: Mix different aspect ratios and sizes deliberately
- **Preserve relationships**: Related content should still feel grouped despite asymmetry

## When to Use

Grid-breaking works best for:
- Hero feature sections where you want visual impact
- Marketing pages that need to stand out
- Portfolio or showcase layouts
- Landing pages competing for attention

For data-heavy interfaces or comparison tables, traditional grids remain appropriate.
