## Why This Matters

Generic design is invisible—it communicates nothing about your product because
it looks like everything else. When you use Inter because it's the default,
purple gradients because AI suggested them, and centered layouts because
they're "safe," you're not designing. You're accepting defaults.

The hallmarks of AI-generated "slop":
- Inter, Roboto, or system fonts (safe, forgettable)
- Purple-to-blue gradients (the new "make it pop")
- Perfectly centered, symmetric layouts
- Excessive border-radius on everything
- Sparkle emojis and buzzwords like "supercharge"

These patterns have become so ubiquitous that they signal *lack* of design
rather than presence of it.

## How to Break Free

### Typography
- Choose fonts with personality: display serifs, geometric sans, monospace
- Pair a distinctive heading font with a refined body font
- Even Google Fonts has characterful options: Playfair Display, Space Grotesk, JetBrains Mono

### Color
- Single bold color with one sharp accent beats a rainbow
- Black, white, and one color is a powerful constraint
- If you use gradients, make them subtle and purposeful

### Layout
- Left-align instead of center-everything
- Asymmetric compositions create visual interest
- Let content dictate structure, not templates

### Details
- Crisp corners (0-4px radius) instead of pill shapes
- Solid borders instead of blur and glow effects
- Structured whitespace instead of even padding everywhere
