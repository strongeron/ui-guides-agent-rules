## Why This Matters

Typography hierarchy is the foundation of visual communication. When headlines and body text share the same generic font, the page becomes a flat wall of text with no clear entry points for the eye.

Intentional font pairing creates immediate visual structure. A distinctive display font for headlines commands attention, while a refined body font ensures comfortable reading. The contrast between them guides users through content naturally.

## Best Practices

- **Display fonts** for headlines: Playfair Display, Fraunces, DM Serif, Space Grotesk
- **Body fonts** for readability: Source Sans, Inter (as body only), IBM Plex Sans, Nunito
- Contrast styles: pair a serif display with a sans-serif body, or a geometric display with a humanist body
- Use `font-display: swap` to prevent invisible text during font loading
- Limit to 2 font families maximum to maintain cohesion

## Common Pairings

| Display (Headlines) | Body (Content) | Style |
|---------------------|----------------|-------|
| Playfair Display | Source Sans 3 | Editorial |
| Space Grotesk | Inter | Technical |
| DM Serif Display | Nunito | Warm |
| Fraunces | IBM Plex Sans | Modern |
