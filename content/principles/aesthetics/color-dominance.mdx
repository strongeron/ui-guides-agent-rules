## Why This Matters

Timid, evenly-distributed palettes create visual confusion. When every color
competes for attention, nothing stands out. A dominant color establishes
hierarchy, while sharp accents draw the eye to key actions and information.

The 60-30-10 rule works: 60% dominant, 30% secondary, 10% accent. But great
design often pushes further—90% dominant with surgical accent placement.

## Best Practices

- Choose one dominant color that defines your visual identity
- Limit accent colors to 1-2 high-contrast choices
- Use accent colors only for primary CTAs and critical status
- Let neutral colors (grays, blacks) support without competing
