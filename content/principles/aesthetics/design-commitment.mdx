## Why This Matters

Timid design choices communicate uncertainty. When you hedge your bets with
pale grays, washed-out colors, and safe neutral tones, the result is forgettable.
Users scroll past without a second thought.

Full design commitment means choosing a direction and executing it with conviction.
A bold dominant color establishes presence. Sharp accents create focal points.
Strong typography commands attention. Every element reinforces the identity.

The difference between mediocre and memorable is often just confidence in execution.

## Signs of Non-Commitment

- Pale grays as the primary palette (safe but lifeless)
- Washed-out colors that lack saturation
- Generic system fonts without character
- Buttons that blend into the background
- No clear visual hierarchy or focal point

## Full Commitment Looks Like

- **Bold dominant color**: Deep blacks, rich brand colors, or confident whites
- **Sharp accents**: Vibrant highlights that draw the eye to key actions
- **Strong typography**: Fonts with character, intentional weight contrast
- **Clear hierarchy**: Obvious primary, secondary, tertiary relationships
- **Consistent execution**: Every element reinforces the chosen direction
