## Why This Matters

There is no universal "right amount" of visual complexity. A brutalist portfolio
site might intentionally feel raw and unpolished. A luxury brand landing page
might layer gradients, textures, and animations for richness. Both are valid.

The problem is mismatch: applying maximalist techniques to minimalist content
(or vice versa) creates cognitive dissonance. A simple note card with layered
shadows, gradient backgrounds, blur effects, and glowing buttons signals
importance that the content does not warrant.

## Best Practices

- Audit your content's inherent complexity before styling
- Simple content (notes, basic forms, status cards) needs restrained styling
- Complex content (dashboards, data visualization, hero sections) can support elaborate treatment
- Count your effects: if layered shadows, gradients, blurs, and animations appear together, ask if each earns its place
- Minimalism requires precision, not absence—every pixel matters

## Complexity Calibration

- **Low complexity content**: Use single shadows, solid colors, simple borders
- **Medium complexity content**: Add subtle gradients, layered shadows, or one animation
- **High complexity content**: Layer effects, use atmospheric backgrounds, orchestrate motion
