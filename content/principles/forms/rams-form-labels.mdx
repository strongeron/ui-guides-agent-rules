<Callout type="quote" title="WCAG 1.3.1 - Level A">
  Form inputs must have labels that describe their purpose. Placeholders alone are not accessible labels - they disappear when typing and are not reliably announced by all screen readers.
</Callout>

## Why This Matters

Labels tell users what information to enter. Without labels, screen reader users hear "edit text" with no context. Visual users also benefit - clicking a label focuses the input. Placeholders are hints, not labels; they vanish on input and have low contrast.

## Implementation

<CodeBlock title="HTML/JSX" language="html">
{`<!-- Explicit label with htmlFor/for -->
<label htmlFor="email">Email address</label>
<input id="email" type="email" />

<!-- Implicit label wrapping input -->
<label>
  Email address
  <input type="email" />
</label>

<!-- With placeholder as additional hint -->
<label htmlFor="email">Email address</label>
<input
  id="email"
  type="email"
  placeholder="you@example.com"
/>

<!-- Visually hidden label for search -->
<label htmlFor="search" className="sr-only">
  Search
</label>
<input id="search" type="search" placeholder="Search..." />`}
</CodeBlock>
